Prompt Engineering

How AI Responds to Prompts

Generative AI like ChatGPT and Claude use two key technologies:

🗣️ Natural Language Processing

Helps AI understand and respond to human language naturally, like talking to another person.

🧠 Machine Learning

Enables AI to learn from your input and improve its responses over time.

📝 Text Prompts

Text prompts are the most common way to interact with AI. The quality of your prompt directly impacts the quality of the response.

✅ Best Practices

1. Be Specific and Clear

✅ Good:

"Write a 3-paragraph summary of Animal Farm by George Orwell with emphasis on motifs."

❌ Bad:

"Tell me about Animal Farm."

2. Give Context

✅ Good:

"I'm a high school student. Explain quantum physics in simple terms."

❌ Bad:

"Explain quantum physics."

3. Use Examples or Structure

✅ Good:

"Create a to-do list for planning a wedding. Format with bullet points."

❌ Bad:

"Help me plan a wedding."

4. Set the Tone or Style

✅ Good:

"Write a funny tweet about coffee addiction."

❌ Bad:

"Write something about coffee."

💡 Impact:

Clear prompts = accurate, useful, and creative responses.
Vague prompts = generic or off-topic answers.

🗣️ Voice Commands

Voice prompts are used in smart assistants like Siri, Alexa, or AI chat apps with voice input. They require different techniques than text prompts.

✅ Best Practices

1. Speak Naturally but Clearly

✅ Good:

"Set a reminder for 3 PM today to call Mom."

❌ Bad:

"Set a Reminder... Mom... later."

2. Use Short, Complete Sentences

✅ Good:

"What's the weather like in London tomorrow?"

❌ Bad:

"London weather?"

💡 Additional Tips:

  • • Avoid background noise for better recognition
  • • Use follow-up questions to maintain context
  • • Example: "Who is Marie Curie?" → "What did she discover?"

💡 Impact:

Clear voice commands = faster, more accurate responses.
Mumbled or fragmented speech = misinterpretation or errors.

🖼️ Visual Prompts

Visual prompts involve uploading or showing images to AI for description, analysis, or generation based on the picture.

✅ Best Practices

1. Use High-Quality Images

✅ Good:

A clear, well-lit photo of a plant for identification.

❌ Bad:

A blurry or dark image.

2. Add Text Context with Images

✅ Good:

"What kind of insect is this?" (with a photo)

❌ Bad:

Just uploading the image with no context.

3. Ask Specific Questions

✅ Good:

"What's the architectural style of this building?"

❌ Bad:

"What do you think?"

4. Combine Visual + Text for Creative Tasks

✅ Good:

"Generate a painting of this dog with a black and white watercolor style."

❌ Bad:

"Make this better."

💡 Impact:

Clear visuals + context = accurate analysis or creative output.
Unclear images or vague prompts = poor results or confusion.

🎯 Types of AI Prompts

Different prompting strategies achieve different results. Understanding these types helps you choose the right approach for your task.

Prompt Type Description Example
Zero-Shot Simple instructions without examples. Quick, general responses. "Summarize this article in 5 bullet points."
Few-Shot Provide examples for AI to mimic. Helps learn structure or tone. "Here are 2 example summaries. Write a third in the same style."
Instructional Direct commands using action verbs. "Write an executive summary under 100 words."
Role-Based AI assumes a specific persona or viewpoint. "You are an MBA professor. Prepare a lecture outline..."
Contextual Include background before asking. Tailors to audience. "This is for undergrads. Rephrase in simpler language."

💡 Final Tips for All Prompt Types

  • • Think like a teacher: Explain as if teaching someone new
  • • Iterate: If the first result isn't perfect, refine and try again
  • • Use formatting: Request bullet points, tables, or sections
  • • Be patient: Complex requests may need multiple attempts

  • ✗ Don't use incomplete sentences
  • ✗ Avoid ambiguous language
